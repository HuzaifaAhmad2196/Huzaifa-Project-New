Speaking and Listening - Principles of Communication ★

The Foundations of Language Development

Children start learning language through listening. They listen to voices, rhythms and sounds way before they utter their first words. When they listen, they are exposed to ideas and vocabulary whereas when they speak they are able to express themselves, ask questions and establish relationships. These are the skills that develop and support each other.

Early Language Development

During early childhood, children will play around with sounds and then they will start combining words into a short sentence. They can say doggy run to describe what they see and slowly take more and complex phrases. Listening is also equally important to assist children to concentrate on instructions, enjoy stories and follow their instructions. Speaking and listening are the basis of literacy, when children cannot maintain a good oral language, they can have a hard time once they start reading and writing.

Building Communication Skills

🗣️ Speaking Skills

Children express themselves, ask questions and establish relationships through speech. They experiment with sounds, combine words into sentences, and gradually develop more complex phrases.

👂 Listening Skills

Children listen to voices, rhythms and sounds before speaking. They concentrate on instructions, enjoy stories, and follow directions. Listening exposes them to ideas and vocabulary.

🔄 Interactive Communication

Speaking and listening develop together and support each other. These skills form the foundation for literacy development and academic success.

📚 Literacy Foundation

Strong oral language skills are essential for reading and writing success. Children who struggle with speaking and listening may have difficulties with literacy.

Daily Activities for Development

💬 Positive Responses

Respond positively to a child whenever they make the first attempts with words. This encourages continued communication attempts.

🔄 Back and Forth Conversations

Have conversations with children back and forth. This models turn-taking and shows that communication is interactive.

📖 Storytelling

Tell children to retell a favourite story. This builds narrative skills and memory while encouraging language use.

🎵 Songs and Music

Sing songs and engage in musical activities. This builds rhythm, memory, and vocabulary while making language learning enjoyable.

Framework Connections

EYLF Outcome 5

🤝

NQS Quality Area 5

Responsive relationships acknowledge that effective communication facilitates wellbeing and learning.

🎯

Daily Practice

These skills may be cultivated by the family on a daily basis through simple, meaningful interactions.

📈

Long-term Benefits

Strong speaking and listening skills create competence and confidence that supports all future learning.

🌍 Supporting Multilingual Children

For children learning multiple languages, speaking and listening skills develop across all languages. Families should use the language they are most comfortable with to provide authentic and rich communication experiences. The first language builds holistic development and supports English acquisition. Mixing languages is a natural stage of growth and should be celebrated as a sign of sophisticated language skills.
